# FureverCare Mood Board

Visual inspiration and references for the FureverCare design direction.

---

## Core Aesthetic Keywords

- **Trustworthy** - Professional, reliable, credible
- **Warm** - Caring, compassionate, friendly
- **Pet-Friendly** - Playful but not childish, inclusive of all pets
- **Emergency-Ready** - Clear, scannable, high-contrast, accessible
- **Modern** - Clean, current, not dated

---

## Color Mood References

### Sage & Coral (Recommended)
- Sage green evokes nature, health, and calm
- Coral brings warmth and energy without being aggressive
- Together: professional veterinary feel + caring pet-owner vibe

**Inspiration sources:**
- Modern veterinary clinic interiors
- Wellness and health apps (Calm, Headspace)
- Pet-friendly hotel aesthetics
- Organic/natural product branding

### Ocean Blue & Sand
- Blue conveys trust, reliability, medical professionalism
- Sand adds warmth and prevents clinical coldness
- Together: like a trusted family doctor's office

**Inspiration sources:**
- Healthcare apps (One Medical, Zocdoc)
- Beach resort calmness
- Scandinavian minimalism

### Teal & Terracotta
- Teal is modern, fresh, tech-forward
- Terracotta grounds it with earthy warmth
- Together: startup energy with approachable feel

**Inspiration sources:**
- Modern fintech apps
- Contemporary home goods (West Elm, CB2)
- Artisanal pet product brands

---

## Typography Mood

### Primary: Rounded Sans-Serif (Nunito)
- Friendly and approachable
- Good readability at all sizes
- Rounded terminals feel soft, not harsh
- Example uses: Headers, brand name, CTAs

### Secondary: Clean Sans-Serif (Inter)
- Excellent screen readability
- Professional without being cold
- Wide language support
- Example uses: Body text, form labels, data

### Personality Guidelines
- **Do**: Use medium to bold weights for confidence
- **Do**: Maintain generous line height for readability
- **Don't**: Use overly playful or script fonts
- **Don't**: Use thin weights that reduce readability

## Imagery Direction

### Style: Flat Illustrations (Preferred)
Modern, consistent illustrations work better than photos because:
- They can represent diverse pets and owners
- They maintain visual consistency
- They don't show distressed or sick animals
- They scale well across devices

**Recommended illustration style:**
- Flat or semi-flat with subtle gradients
- Soft, rounded shapes
- Limited color palette (3-4 colors from brand palette)
- Consistent line weights (2-3px)
- Warm, positive scenarios

### Reference Illustration Styles
1. **unDraw** (undraw.co) - Clean, flat, customizable
2. **Humaaans** style - Simple, friendly figures
3. **Blush Design** - Customizable illustration packs
4. **Craftwork** - Premium illustration sets

### Photography (Secondary Option)
If photos are used:
- Bright, natural lighting
- Happy, healthy pets
- Warm indoor or outdoor settings
- Human-pet connection moments
- Avoid: clinical settings, distressed animals

**Photo references:**
- Pet insurance marketing (healthy, happy vibe)
- Lifestyle pet brand photography
- Veterinary clinic waiting room imagery (calm, modern)

## UI Pattern Inspiration

### Cards & Containers
- Rounded corners (12-16px radius)
- Subtle shadows (not harsh drop shadows)
- White backgrounds with colored accents
- Clear visual hierarchy

**References:**
- Apple Health app cards
- Notion blocks
- Linear issue cards

### Emergency/Alert Information
- Left border accent for categorization
- Color-coded severity (red for critical, yellow for warning)
- High contrast for accessibility
- Large, readable text

**References:**
- Medical alert bracelets
- Hospital patient wristbands
- Medical record systems (Epic, Cerner UI)

### Forms & Inputs
- Large touch targets (44px minimum)
- Clear labels above fields
- Inline validation with friendly messages
- Progress indicators for multi-step flows

**References:**
- Stripe checkout flow
- Typeform form design
- Apple system inputs

## Icon Style

### Recommended: Line Icons
- 2px stroke weight
- Rounded caps and joins
- 24x24 base grid
- Consistent visual weight

**Icon sets to reference:**
- Lucide Icons (lucide.dev)
- Heroicons (heroicons.com)
- Phosphor Icons (phosphoricons.com)
- Feather Icons (feathericons.com)

### Icon Categories Needed
- Pet types (dog, cat, bird, rabbit, etc.)
- Medical (pill, syringe, stethoscope, alert)
- Actions (add, edit, share, scan)
- Navigation (home, profile, settings)
- Status (checkmark, warning, error)

---

## Motion & Animation

### Principles
- **Subtle**: Animations should enhance, not distract
- **Purposeful**: Every animation communicates something
- **Fast**: Keep durations short (150-300ms)

### Where to Use Motion
- Page transitions (subtle fade)
- Loading states (smooth spinners)
- Success confirmations (gentle check animation)
- Error shakes (attention without alarm)
- Card hover states (subtle lift)

### What to Avoid
- Bouncy or playful animations (can feel unserious)
- Long delays or durations
- Animation for animation's sake

---

## Accessibility Notes

### Color Contrast
- All text must meet WCAG AA (4.5:1 minimum)
- Error states can't rely on color alone
- Provide patterns or icons alongside colors

### Touch Targets
- Minimum 44x44px for all interactive elements
- Adequate spacing between tap targets
- Clear focus states for keyboard navigation

### Readability
- Body text minimum 16px
- Line height 1.5-1.6 for body text
- Maximum line length ~65-75 characters

## Summary: The FureverCare Feel

Imagine walking into a modern veterinary clinic that feels like a spa:
- Clean and professional, but warm
- Organized and efficient, but not cold
- Focused on care, not bureaucracy
- Trustworthy with your pet's health info
- Welcoming to all pets and owners

The design should make users feel:
1. **Confident** their pet's info is secure and accessible
2. **Calm** during stressful emergency situations
3. **Cared for** as pet owners who love their animals
4. **In control** of their pet's health information
